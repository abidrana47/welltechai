01

Overview

WellTechAI CIC ("we", "us", "our") operates www.welltechai.co.uk and related business enquiry channels. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle personal data when you visit our website, contact us, request a demo or pilot, or otherwise interact with us directly.

This page is written for a UK-based startup and is intended to make the key points clear: what data we collect, why we collect it, how we store it, who we share it with, and how you can ask us to delete it.

UK GDPR Principles

We aim to process personal data in line with the core UK GDPR principles: lawfulness, fairness and transparency; purpose limitation; data minimisation; accuracy; storage limitation; integrity and confidentiality; and accountability.

If you use a WellTechAI CIC solution through an NHS trust, integrated care system, employer, university, or another healthcare provider, that organisation may give you a separate privacy notice explaining its own controller responsibilities.

05

Lawful Bases for Processing

Under UK GDPR, we need a lawful basis before we process personal data. Depending on the context, we may rely on the following Article 6 bases:

Consent - Article 6(1)(a)

For optional marketing communications, optional cookie categories if introduced in future, and certain uses of special category data where explicit consent is appropriate.

Contract - Article 6(1)(b)

When we need to take steps at your request before entering into a contract, or when we need to deliver contracted services to a customer.

Legal Obligation - Article 6(1)(c)

When we must comply with applicable laws, regulatory requests, safeguarding obligations, accounting rules, or formal complaint handling obligations.

Legitimate Interests - Article 6(1)(f)

For operating our website, managing business relationships, responding to non-sensitive enquiries, improving our services, preventing fraud, and protecting our systems where those interests are not overridden by your rights.

06

Health and Special Category Data

Health information is generally treated as special category data under UK GDPR. That means it needs extra protection and an additional Article 9 condition for processing.

Important

Please do not send health records, NHS numbers, crisis information, or other sensitive personal data through the public website enquiry form unless we have explicitly asked you to use a secure channel.

  • Where we process special category data directly, we will identify both a lawful basis under Article 6 and an appropriate condition under Article 9.
  • In many customer deployments, the relevant healthcare organisation decides the purpose of processing and provides the primary privacy information to end users.
  • Where appropriate, additional safeguards may include access controls, role separation, contractual restrictions, confidentiality obligations, and clinical governance controls.

We store personal data in electronic systems used to operate our website, manage enquiries, run email communications, and support customer relationships or service delivery.

AreaHow we protect it
Website and form submissionsWe use controlled systems and restrict access to people who need the information to handle your enquiry.
Email and business recordsWe keep data in business communication tools and internal records with authentication, role-based access, and administrative controls where appropriate.
Service environmentsWhere services are deployed for customers, storage location, security controls, backup arrangements, and access rules may be set out in customer contracts, security schedules, and deployment documents.
  • We take reasonable technical and organisational measures designed to protect personal data against loss, misuse, unauthorised access, alteration, or disclosure.
  • These measures may include encrypted transmission, access control, least-privilege permissions, password management, audit logging where appropriate, and secure deletion processes.
  • Where suppliers or support arrangements involve access outside the UK, we use appropriate contractual or legal safeguards required by UK data protection law.
  • No internet transmission or storage environment is completely risk-free, so we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Who We Share Data With

We Do Not Sell Personal Data

We do not sell your personal data and we do not share it for third-party advertising purposes unrelated to our services.

Service providers and processors

We may share data with trusted providers who help us operate the website, host systems, send or receive email, store records, provide security services, or support customer delivery. Where required, they act under contracts containing confidentiality and data protection obligations.

Customers and prospective partners

If you contact us on behalf of an organisation, we may share relevant details internally and with appropriate colleagues or representatives involved in the same pilot, procurement, or project discussion.

Professional advisers and regulators

We may share information with legal advisers, auditors, insurers, regulators, law enforcement, courts, or public authorities where necessary to comply with law, investigate issues, protect rights, or respond to formal requests.

Corporate transactions

If our business structure changes, personal data may be disclosed to investors, funders, acquirers, or advisers as part of due diligence or a transaction, subject to appropriate confidentiality protections.

09

Data Retention

We keep personal data only for as long as it is reasonably needed for the purpose it was collected, unless a longer period is required by law, contract, or regulatory expectation.

Data typeTypical retention approach
Website enquiry emails and contact form submissionsUsually up to 24 months after the last meaningful contact, unless needed longer for an active opportunity, dispute, or legal reason.
Marketing preferencesUntil you unsubscribe, withdraw consent, or we determine the record is no longer needed.
Business and contract recordsFor the life of the relationship and a reasonable period afterwards for governance, accounting, tax, legal, or audit purposes.
Deployment data processed for customersAs defined by the customer contract, deployment documentation, and any applicable healthcare or regulatory retention obligations.
Technical and security logsFor as long as reasonably needed for security, troubleshooting, and operational monitoring.

When data is no longer needed, we delete it, anonymise it, or securely archive it only where continued retention is legally justified.

Data Security

  • We use reasonable technical and organisational security measures appropriate to the type of data and the context in which it is processed.
  • These measures may include secure transmission, access controls, authentication, backups, monitoring, and least-privilege access management.

Your UK GDPR Rights

Depending on the circumstances, you may have the right to access, correct, delete, restrict, move, or object to the use of your personal data. You may also withdraw consent where consent is the basis for processing.

We usually respond within one month. If a request is complex, we may extend that period by up to two further months, but we will let you know.

Article 15

Access

Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you.

Article 16

Rectification

Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.

Article 17

Erasure

Request deletion of your personal data, subject to legal obligations.

Article 18

Restrict Processing

Limit how we use your data while a request is reviewed.

Article 20

Portability

Receive your data in a portable, machine-readable format.

Article 21

Object

Object to processing for marketing or legitimate interest purposes.

We do not use website enquiry data for solely automated decisions with legal or similarly significant effects.

12

How to Delete Your Data

If you want us to delete personal data we control, email info@welltechai.co.uk and include Data Deletion Request in the subject line.

  1. Tell us the name, email address, organisation, or other details that help us find your record.
  2. Tell us what you want deleted, for example a contact enquiry, marketing record, or previous correspondence.
  3. We may ask for proof of identity before acting on the request so we do not delete the wrong person's data.
  4. We will review whether we can delete the data immediately or whether we must keep some of it to comply with law, defend legal claims, prevent fraud, or meet contractual obligations.
  5. If we cannot fully delete data, we will explain why and, where possible, restrict its use instead.
If the Data Came Through an NHS or Other Customer Deployment

If your data was collected by an NHS trust, university, employer, charity, or another organisation using a WellTechAI CIC service, that organisation may be the main controller. In that case, you may need to contact that organisation first so the request is handled correctly.
